Background

AkuteHealth is a healthcare mobile application developed for iOS devices which medical patients can use to track, manage, and share their medical records by adding their doctor's patient portal. In essence, it is a Personal Health Record (PHR) solution delivered to medical patients in the form of a mobile application. It is available to download via the App Store.

Problem

The application is still in its early stages of development and while there are some exciting potential features planned for the future (data visualizations, easy sharing options), the current available version is a no-frills, bare-bones version with little to no visual hierarchy or design components being used, resulting in poor usability and user experience.

Solution

We decided to focus on addressing the usability issues and thus, as part of the design recommendations, presented a 4-point action plan to the developer to improve the usability of the application.

Client Kickoff

The initial kickoff meeting consisted of having the developer of the application, Sharud Agarwal introducing us briefly to the application, walking us through its features and future development plans via a remote video call. By the end of it, we established two important project objectives:

  1. Understand the pain points that users have accessing their health information
  2. Nudging AkuteHealth in the right direction towards alleviating those pain points

User Research

Competitive Analysis

For our competitive analysis, we decided to look at five different competitors (3 direct, 2 indirect), all of which were iOS mobile applications. We identified the strengths and weaknesses for each of these, consolidated them and prepared a final chart for analysis.

On analysis, we found that the direct competitors focused on clear presentation of medical information. This makes for an easy way to acquire the necessary medical information that is important to the user. However, most of the direct competitors lacked basic functionality and suffered with long wait times for getting updated information. Akute Health could get ahead of this by having the functionality work well and consistently.

Screening, Recruitment and Interviews

Since the user demographic for such an application was very specific, we decided to create a screener first in order to help us identify the best participants and ease the recruitment process. Affinity Mapping

After we finished our user interviews, we decided to conduct an affinity mapping session to group the feedback from the interviews, identify common themes and patterns and generate insights from those. From each category, these were the insights that stood out the most to us, which formed the basis of our design recommendations.

Recommendations

By the end of our analysis from interviews and research, we narrowed down our recommendations to an actionable 4-point plan that we presented to Sharud.

Reflection and Lessons Learned

This project was the first time where I got to wear the hat of a user researcher and focus on solving a problem only through that lens, without worrying about designing a potential solution. Because it was a more research-driven project that focused on improving overall usability of an application, it allowed me to follow a robust research methodology from start to finish.

It also introduced me to the digital healthcare world where time-critical applications take centerstage and it is important to know how to cut through the medical verbiage to display the most important information to the user. Context in design is always important.
