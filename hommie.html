Problem Space

As a newcomer moving in to a new city or place, looking for housing and roommate(s) can be a very distressing experience. One has to find compatible people to live with while also making sure to find the right place with the right set of facilities that match their needs and expectations.

Design Goal: How do we design a cohesive and engaging solution that streamlines the current experience to make it more enjoyable and alleviates the usersâ€™ pain?

Solution

Hommie is an integrated housing website that brings together the fundamentally different and often disconnected experiences of finding housing (new and temporary sublets) and roommates under one place.

User Research

Competitive Analysis

For our competitive analysis, we decided to look at four services which were focused on finding either roommates, housing or both. Our goal with this activity was to identify the strategies that these services used to accomplish these tasks and also to identify the strengths and weaknesses that would help us inform our own design. While Facebook wasn't a standalone website, it still serves as a popular platform for having dedicated groups for college students to find housing and roommates. We wanted to explore that space and see what made Facebook a popular choice among students. We decided to categorize the findings from the analysis into three broad categories - Information Architecture, Website Content and Visual Design.

User Interviews

The next step was to conduct in-person user interviews. We interviewed a total of 8 people, asking them about their current housing and roommate searching experience and to better understand their needs, pain points and expectations. We then collected our interview notes and brainstormed using an affinity diagram exercise to identify patterns and generate insights.

Key findings from the interviews were:

  • Price and location are the two most important factors for people to decide housing.
  • People find it difficult to decide a roommate and trust random strangers, especially if they have never met them before.
  • For most people (6 out of 8 in the sample size), it is more important to have compatible roommate(s) with similar interests and habits than having a good housing place.
  • For a sublease situation, roommates of the subletter (person granting sublease) said they would like some involvement in the selection process of the sublessee (person taking sublease).

Information Architecture

After conducting our user research, we had to now think about the website organization and content design for the website. There were however two main challenges that we faced at this stage:

  1. Lack of similar, already existing systems that we could use as reference and build upon
  2. Defining the scale of the project for an integrated system that would serve as a three-in-one solution

In an effort to tackle these challenges, we conducted these IA activities to create a functional, robust and efficient information architecture for Hommie.

Content Inventory

Creating a content inventory helped us build a solid foundation for our project in terms of the levels of navigation we would aim for, the contents on each level and the overall project hierarchy.

We defined four levels of navigation for the content and the initial structure of our website. We decided to have a total of six global navigation items - Home, Look for housing, Look for Roommates, Look for Sublease, Profile and Messages. While the global navigation items largely remained unchanged until the end, the sublevels within each changed significantly as our project continued to grow and evolve.

Card Sorting

Next, we decided to conduct a card sorting session. With this activity, we wanted to validate our hypotheses about the site structure and information and what potential users had in mind for a project like this. We went for a hyrid card sort since we wanted the participants to have a basic sense of understanding for the project and also new suggested categories would help us think about any missed information.

Key findings included:

  • There were some cards such as Search, Sort and Filter which the participants found difficult to fit under one category
  • Participants shared that searching for roommates and housing is a non-linear process, one doesnâ€™t necessarily have to follow another
  • Some information should be accessible anonymously, without the need of a registered user account

Sitemap

One final step towards solidifying the information architecture for our website was to create a sitemap to give shape to our hierarchy that we defined as part of the content inventory.

Designing and Prototyping

Sketches and Lo-fi Wireframes

Once we had conducted our research and analysis, we started working on sketches for the UI screens. We then replicated these sketches into low fidelity wireframes to test the product idea with a few users. At this stage, we decided to not flesh out the content since we just wanted some initial feedback on the whole design concept.

Hi-fi Prototyping

For our high fidelity mockups, we fleshed out our wireframes and developed a clean and modern design for the website. We decided to use a calming color palette, mainly consisting of shades of green and blue. We conducted our first round of usability testing with this set of prototypes and we received some valuable feedback that informed our design and helped us refine it further going forward.

Usability Testing

Key Changes Made and Design Decisions Taken

1. User Tags Location

Participants could not associate which tags belonged to whom in a group post. Thus, we decided to create separate sections for each post member with their matching tags to eliminate confusion.

2. Filters For Housing

Participants were confused by the wording of a few filters and felt few important filters were missing. We changed the wording to make it less ambiguous and incorporated more filters.

3. CTA Behavior

Participants pointed out that the 'List My Place' button seemed active by default. We changed the active and inactive states of the button and pushed it towards the right to separate it from the two Search buttons.

4. Card Design

Participants were confused by the icon choices for occupied rooms and wished to see age on profile cards. We incorporated the suggestions to reflect those choices better and also added a 'Sublease' label to each sublease post card for more clarity.

Reflection and Lessons Learned

This was a challenging and ambitious project for me to work on. It was difficult to design for a product that performed two mammoth tasks of finding roommate and housing under one roof. This led to a lot of scoping issues in the beginning. We had our moments of uncertainty, where we did not know where the project was heading. But we even though it may have its shortcomings, the idea for this project stemmed from a common problem that so many international students (including us) have faced or continue to face.

This taught me the importance of specificity of problem when designing for any project. While one cannot create a solution for every problem, we should definitely create a solution for a specific problem and ensure that it is effective. Also, I would have liked to have conducted a second round of usability testing after making the changes in design if we had more time.
